CHUCK
PALAHNIUK

Charles Michael "Chuck" Palahniuk (born February 21, 1962) is an American novelist who describes his work as transgressional fiction. He has published 19 novels, three nonfiction books, two graphic novels, and two adult coloring books, as well as several short stories. His first published novel was Fight Club, which was adapted into a film of the same title.

Early Career

He wrote for the local newspaper for a short while but then began working for Freightliner Trucks as a diesel mechanic, continuing until his writing career took off. During that time, he wrote manuals on fixing trucks and had a stint as a journalist, a job to which he did not return until after he became a successful novelist. Palahniuk quit his job as a journalist in 1988. He performed volunteer work for a homeless shelter and volunteered at a hospice.

Fight Club

After his first novel, Invisible Monsters, was rejected, Palahniuk began work on his first published novel, Fight Club. Initially a short story, it was expanded into a full novel and published in 1996. The novel received positive reviews and some awards, although it had a short shelf life until the film adaptation revived its popularity.
